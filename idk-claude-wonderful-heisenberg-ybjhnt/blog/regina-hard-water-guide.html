Water quality

Regina's hard water: what it's doing to your home

By the Wascana Plumbing crew · Updated October 2025 · 6 min read

If you've ever wondered why your kettle furs up in a month, your shower glass never looks clean, and your soap won't lather — it's not you, it's the water. Regina sits on some of the hardest municipal water in Canada, and it quietly works on your plumbing every single day.

What "hard water" actually means

Hard water simply means water with a lot of dissolved minerals — mostly calcium and magnesium. Regina's water is treated and perfectly safe to drink; it's just mineral-rich. As that water heats up or evaporates, those minerals drop out and stick to whatever they touch as a chalky white scale. Over months and years, that scale adds up.

The signs you already know

  • White, crusty buildup on faucets, aerators and shower heads
  • Spots on dishes and a permanent film on the glass shower door
  • Soap and shampoo that won't lather, and skin that feels dry
  • Stiff, dingy laundry that wears out faster
  • A kettle or coffee maker that scales up in weeks

The expensive part you can't see

The cosmetic stuff is annoying. The costly part happens inside your plumbing and appliances:

  • Water heaters: scale settles to the bottom of the tank, insulating the burner from the water. Your heater works harder, costs more to run, gets noisy (that rumbling is scale), and dies years early.
  • Fixtures and valves: scale clogs aerators, seizes shut-off valves and shortens the life of every faucet and cartridge in the house.
  • Appliances: dishwashers and washing machines scale up internally and lose efficiency. Tankless water heaters are especially sensitive and need regular descaling here.
We pull water heaters in Regina with so much scale in the bottom you could stand a spoon up in it. Soft water can genuinely add years to a tank's life.

Is a water softener worth it?

For most Regina homes, yes — it's one of the few upgrades that pays you back. A softener swaps the calcium and magnesium for a tiny amount of sodium, so the scale never forms in the first place. You'll notice it within days: softer laundry, easier cleaning, real lather, and spotless dishes. Over the years, you'll feel it in fewer fixture repairs and a water heater that lasts.

A quality softener, professionally installed, typically runs from about $1,150 in our area, plus a small amount of salt every month or two. If you're already replacing a scaled-up water heater, it's the natural time to add one and protect the new tank from day one.

Not sure how hard your water is, or whether a softener fits your home? We'll test it and give you a straight recommendation — no pressure. Book a water check or call (306) 555-0147.

If you'd rather not soften

A softener isn't the only option. At minimum, descale your shower heads and aerators a few times a year (soak them in white vinegar overnight), flush your water heater annually to clear sediment, and descale a tankless unit on schedule. It won't stop hard water, but it slows the damage.
