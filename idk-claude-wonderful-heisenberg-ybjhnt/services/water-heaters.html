Repair or replace?

We'll tell you straight

Not every water heater needs replacing. A failed thermocouple, element or anode rod is often a quick, affordable fix. But if your tank is 12+ years old, rumbling, rusty at the base or leaking, throwing money at it rarely pays off. We show you what we find and let you decide.

  • No hot water or it runs out fast — element, thermostat or sediment
  • Rusty or smelly hot water — usually a spent anode rod
  • Popping or rumbling — sediment buildup from Regina's hard water
  • Water pooling at the base — the tank's failing; replace before it floods
