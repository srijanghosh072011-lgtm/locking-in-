When to call right away

Don't wait this one out

A small leak can soak a ceiling in an hour and a frozen line can split a wall open. If you're seeing any of these, call now — the faster we're there, the less it costs to put right.

  • Burst or split pipe — water spraying, dripping through a ceiling, or pooling on the floor
  • Frozen lines — no water at a tap on an outside wall during a cold snap
  • Sewage backing up into a floor drain, tub or basement
  • No water at all, or a water heater leaking onto the floor
  • Smell of gas near a water heater or gas line — leave the house first, then call

Emergency FAQ

Quick answers when you're stressed

Our target is 60 minutes anywhere in Regina, and we're often faster. During an extreme cold snap, when half the city's pipes freeze at once, we triage by severity — active flooding and no-heat situations go first.

Shut off the water at the main valve (usually in the basement near the meter) and, for a water-heater leak, turn off its supply too. Move anything valuable away from the water. Then call us — we'll guide you through the rest on the phone.

There's a modest after-hours call-out fee, and we tell you that number before we dispatch — never a surprise. The repair itself is a flat, approved price regardless of the hour.
