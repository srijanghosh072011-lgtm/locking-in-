How to unclog a drain without wrecking your pipes

Most blocked sinks can be cleared with things you already own. Here are five plumber-approved methods — in the order we’d try them — plus the one “fix” to avoid.

1. Start with boiling water

For a slow kitchen drain, the culprit is usually congealed grease and soap. Boil a full kettle and pour it down in two or three stages, leaving a few seconds between each pour. It melts the gunk and flushes it through. (Skip this one if you have PVC pipes that are already loose — very hot water can soften old joints.)

2. Try a plunger — the right way

A cup plunger works on sinks, not just toilets. Fill the basin with enough water to cover the plunger lip, block the overflow hole with a damp cloth, and plunge firmly 15–20 times. The seal and the water are what create the pressure — without them you’re just making noise.

Pro tip: Got a double sink? Block the second drain with a wet rag first, or the pressure escapes out the other side.

3. Baking soda and vinegar

A natural option for light blockages and odours: pour in half a cup of baking soda, then half a cup of white vinegar. Cover the drain, wait 15 minutes while it fizzes, then flush with hot water. It won’t shift a solid blockage, but it’s great for maintenance.

4. Clean the P-trap

The U-shaped pipe under the sink (the “P-trap”) catches everything. Put a bucket underneath, unscrew the two slip nuts by hand, and clear out the debris. This fixes a huge number of blockages — and finds lost rings. Reassemble snugly and run the water to check for leaks.

5. Use a drain snake

If the blockage is deeper, a hand-operated drain snake (auger) reaches what a plunger can’t. Feed it in until you hit resistance, turn the handle to hook or break up the clog, then pull it back out. Go gently — forcing it can scratch or crack older pipes.

The one to avoid: chemical drain cleaners. They’re harsh on pipes and seals, dangerous to handle, and often just sit on top of a solid blockage. We replace plenty of corroded pipes because of them.
