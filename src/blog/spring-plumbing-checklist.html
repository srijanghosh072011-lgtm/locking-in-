Your spring plumbing checklist

Fifteen minutes now can save you a flooded weekend later. Run through these ten checks as the weather warms up.

  1. Test your main shut-off valve. Make sure you can find it and turn it. In an emergency, seconds count.
  2. Check under every sink. Feel for damp, look for green/white corrosion on fittings, and tighten slow drips.
  3. Look for running toilets. Add a few drops of food colouring to the cistern — if colour appears in the bowl without flushing, the flapper is leaking.
  4. Inspect your hot water system. Check the install date, look for rust or pooling water, and listen for rumbling (sediment).
  5. Clean drain stoppers. Pull out and clear hair and gunk from bathroom and shower drains.
  6. Test water pressure. Suddenly weak pressure can signal a hidden leak or a failing regulator.
  7. Check outdoor taps and hoses. After winter, look for splits and slow drips at garden taps.
  8. Clear gutters and downpipes. Blocked drainage sends water where it shouldn’t go.
  9. Flush rarely-used drains. Guest bathrooms can dry out — run water to refill traps and stop sewer smells.
  10. Book a pro check-up. An annual plumbing health-check catches what the eye misses.
